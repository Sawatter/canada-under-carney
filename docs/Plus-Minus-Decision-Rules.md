# Plus/Minus Grade Decision Rules

**Purpose:** Explicit rules for when a dimension receives a plus, minus, or straight letter grade. Prevents false precision on weak evidence.

---

## Rule

Every dimension starts at the straight letter grade. Plus or minus is added only when specific conditions are met.

### Plus (+) requires ALL of:
1. The dimension meets its letter-grade threshold
2. At least one primary indicator shows measurable positive momentum toward the next letter grade
3. No material caveat or confounder undermines the positive signal

**Example:** Immigration at C+ — the correction is executing (meets C threshold) AND temp arrivals down 53% with population declining (measurable momentum) AND no service failure documented yet (no undermining caveat).

### Minus (-) requires ANY of:
1. The dimension meets its letter-grade threshold but with documented accounting caveats or methodology questions
2. A single strong sub-file masks weakness elsewhere in the dimension
3. The evidence base for the grade is thinner than for other dimensions at the same letter

**Example:** Defence & Trade at A- — NATO 2% met (meets A threshold) BUT partly through accounting reclassification AND Canada placed in NATO's bottom third at exactly 2.0% (documented caveats).

### Straight letter requires:
1. The dimension meets its letter-grade threshold
2. Neither plus nor minus conditions are met
3. OR: the evidence is too thin to justify plus/minus precision (applies to probationary dimensions)

## Decision process

For each monthly update, plus/minus assignment follows this checklist:

1. Determine the letter grade from the scoring sheet thresholds
2. Check: is any primary indicator showing measurable positive momentum? → candidate for plus
3. Check: are there documented caveats, accounting questions, or masking effects? → candidate for minus
4. Check: is this a probationary dimension? → whole letter only
5. Document the plus/minus rationale in the release log
